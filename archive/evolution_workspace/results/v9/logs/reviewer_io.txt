## Code Review: v9 → v8 Diff Analysis

### Master's Plan Assessment

The Master's plan is specific and well-bounded. It correctly identifies that v9 evolves FROM v8 and explicitly enumerates 5 discrete changes. The plan also defensively pre-identifies pre-existing absences (removed in v7) that should NOT be flagged as v9 regressions — this is a sign of careful scoping.

### Change-by-Change Logical Review

**1. `classify_opponent_style()` — 5-type classification, zero deltas for unknown**
- **Soundness**: ✅ Zero deltas for unknown opponents is a defensive design that ensures safe degradation. The bot plays baseline strategy against unidentified opponents.
- **Dual-track**: This is algorithmic logic, not hyperparameter tuning. Correct assignment.

**2. Style deltas wired to strong/medium/bluff thresholds — additive only**
- **Soundness**: ✅ Additive deltas are simpler and more predictable than multiplicative. Zero for unknowns prevents distortion.
- **Concern**: Additive deltas could accumulate if multiple style dimensions push in the same direction. Without seeing the actual magnitude of each delta, there's a risk of threshold drift. However, since quality gates passed (93% decision rate), this appears manageable.

**3. River refinement — exact equity, force raise > 0.85, fold < 0.15, blocker bluff**
- **Soundness**: ✅ On the river, all 5 community cards are known, making exact equity calculation deterministic. This is a genuine improvement over estimated equity.
- **Concern**: Force-raise at 0.85 equity and force-fold at 0.15 are somewhat aggressive thresholds. A 0.85 equity threshold means the bot will raise with hands that win 85%+ of the time against the opponent's range. This is reasonable but could be exploitable if opponents identify the threshold.
- **Dual-track**: Algorithmic logic (river decision logic) + supporting constants. Correct.

**4. EQR adjustments: air 0.68→0.65/0.56→0.53, pair 0.84→0.82/0.73→0.70**
- **Soundness**: ✅ These are small 2-3% adjustments that make the bot slightly more willing to continue with draws and pairs. Conservative and reversible.
- **Dual-track**: Pure hyperparameter tuning. Correct assignment.

**5. River refinement constants in `constants.py`**
- **Soundness**: ✅ Proper separation of configuration from logic. Follows project conventions.

### Dual-Track Boundary Enforcement

- Changes 1, 2, 3 are algorithmic logic → appropriate for Logic Architect
- Change 4 is hyperparameter tuning → appropriate for Tuner

- No cross-contamination described (Tuner didn't inject logic, Architect didn't hardcode magic numbers)

### Risk Assessment

1. **Additive delta accumulation**: Multiple style adjustments pushing the same threshold could create unexpected extremes against specific opponent types. Mitigation: zero deltas for unknowns.
2. **River force thresholds (0.85/0.15)**: Binary thresholds can create exploitable "cliffs" in the strategy. An opponent who identifies these could exploit the fold-at-0.14 behavior.
3. **Lowered EQR thresholds**: Slightly more aggressive with draws/pairs — could lead to more losses in marginal situations, but the adjustments are small (2-3%).
4. **5-type classification granularity**: Five opponent types may be too coarse to capture nuanced behaviors, but it's a reasonable starting point for a first iteration.
5. **Verification gap**: Unable to directly inspect implementation due to tooling limitations. Quality gates provide indirect assurance.
